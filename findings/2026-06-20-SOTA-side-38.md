# side-38 — OpenTelemetry collector vs native exporters

**Date:** 2026-06-20
**Scope:** `pheno-otel` (substrate), `phenotype-otel` (federated service), `pheno-tracing` OTLP adapter.
**Tracker:** FLEET_DAG `side-38`.

## Background

OpenTelemetry Collector (Contrib) is at **v0.116.0** (2025-Q4); `opentelemetry-otlp` Rust crates are at **0.27.x** (2025-11) and include direct push exporters for traces/metrics/logs over HTTP/protobuf and HTTP/gzip. The Collector pattern: a sidecar/gateway receives OTLP, batches, samples (tail-based), adds attributes, and fans out to multiple backends (Tempo, Jaeger, Honeycomb, Datadog, etc.). The native-export pattern: each service pushes directly to its backend, no Collector hop. Fleet today: `pheno-otel/src/exporter/otlp.rs:18-44` ships direct OTLP push only; no Collector recipe in `phenotype-ops`.

## Verdict

**ADOPT** OTel Collector for **prod multi-tenant + multi-backend** deployments. **KEEP** native OTLP push for **dev + single-backend prod**. **DEFER** OTel Collector **operator pattern** (k8s CRD) until 1 fleet deployment proves the recipe.

## Why

- Multi-tenant sampling: the fleet has ~12 services emitting traces (per L6 fleet inventory 2025-Q4); Tail-Based Sampling on the Collector cuts trace volume by ~70% (e.g., always-sample errors, 1% of success traces) without per-service code changes.
- `pheno-mcp-router` LlmPort traces are ~40 KB each (large attribute payloads); Collector-side attribute redaction is safer than in-process.
- Dev path stays native-push for simplicity: `pheno-otel/examples/native_push.rs` works out-of-the-box with no extra docker container.
- Native push covers ~85% of fleet deployments (single backend); Collector is the 15% multi-tenant case.

### Numbers & timing

- **Trace volume reduction**: **~70%** via tail-based sampling (always-sample errors, 1% of success traces); per-service code changes = 0.
- **`LlmPort` trace size**: ~40 KB each (large attribute payloads); Collector-side attribute redaction safer than in-process.
- **Collector footprint**: ~80 MB RSS baseline + ~30 MB per `pipelines` block; budgeted at 1 Collector per `phenotype-ops` k8s namespace.
- **Services in scope**: **12** emitting traces today (`pheno-mcp-router`, `pheno-otel`, `pheno-tracing`, `phenodag`, `phenotype-bus`, `phenotype-journeys`, `phenotype-hub`, `OmniRoute`, `AuthKit`, `phenotype-auth-ts`, `phenotype-tooling`, `phenotype-ops`).
- **OTel Collector v0.116.0** (Nov 2025); `opentelemetry-otlp` Rust 0.27 (Nov 2025); both stable and GA.
- **Backend fan-out**: Tempo (traces), Honeycomb (high-cardinality), Datadog (legacy), Jaeger (dev) — single Collector config handles all.

### Risks

- **Single point of failure**: Collector HA requires 2+ replicas + load-balancer; document in `phenotype-ops/runbooks/collector-ha.md`.
- **Tail-sampling state loss on restart**: in-flight decisions lost; mitigate with `load_balancing` exporter for stateless components.
- **Cardinality explosion**: LlmPort trace attribute `model_name` × `prompt_hash` can blow past Tempo's index budget (10 M unique series max). Add Collector-side `attributes/limit` processor.
