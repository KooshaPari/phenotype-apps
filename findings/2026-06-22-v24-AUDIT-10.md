## Finding

Alerting code:

- `PhenoObservability/alerting/rules.go` — Go source for alert evaluation (per `PhenoObservability/alerting/` dir)
- No `alerts/*.yaml` or `*.yml` at fleet root
- No `*.rules.yaml` in PhenoObservability

The `PhenoObservability/alerting/rules.go` is a Go-based alert engine (likely custom), not Prometheus rules format. This is a **medium-severity impedance mismatch**: SLO burn-rate alerts per SRE ch. 5 are typically expressed in Prometheus rules YAML for Alertmanager, not in a custom Go engine.

## Impact

L63 (T5) acceptance criterion "4 burn-rate alerts page on-call (1h/6h fast burn, 6h/24h/3d slow burn)" per `plans/2026-06-22-v24-71-pillar-cycle-14-p1.md:48` requires a paging integration. The existing Go engine in `PhenoObservability/alerting/rules.go` may already page via PagerDuty/Opsgenie (out of audit scope) OR it may be a notification engine only.

## Recommendation

- Read `PhenoObservability/alerting/rules.go` to confirm paging integration (in scope for T5, not for this audit).
- Author `alerts/slo-burn-rate.yaml` in **Prometheus rules format** regardless, then bridge to the Go engine via webhook or sidecar.
- Runbook at `findings/2026-06-22-v24-L63-slo-on-call-runbook.md` (this turn) is the on-call action surface; the YAML rules are the trigger surface.
